# Reflect: Falsifying the Probe Output

## Continue
We must continue attacking the definitions of our mechanical loops. The Operator's rub on the "most important output" of a Probe ensures we don't accidentally build a factory for useless execution trails.

## Start
We must start recognizing that the most important output of a Probe is **Falsification of the Condition**, not the guarantee of an execution trail. 
While a successful atomic Probe *does* yield exactly one invariant (if it yields more, it is a macro node that requires further decomposition), its highest-value outcome is discovering that an assumed condition is false—allowing us to cleanly discard the trail and avoid writing dead code.

## Stop
We must stop treating Probes as mere "execution prep." A Probe is not a guaranteed runway for a `plan-execute-reflect` trail. It is an active structural gate. If the condition is false, the execution trail must be aborted.
