## Survives (the core, measured)

Cairn's plain README carries zero footnote apparatus and loses nothing by it: each
knife line holds its full refutation-extension inline, and even the formal sibling
needed no footnote indirection — extensions, arguments, and pre-answers all fit as
inline prose. The contrast with bond is real: **bond's plain lens still needs 8
footnotes (16 references) after its register split** — the split moved the formal
surface out but could not shed the footnote mechanism.

"Tighter in scope" survives in this precise sense: cairn's claims are
**self-contained** — no claim leans on a frozen defined term or a scope carve-out
outside its own sentence. Bond's surviving footnotes are exactly that apparatus: term
definitions its claims depend on (`[^oracle]`, `[^model]`), scope-boundary carve-outs
(`[^split]`, `[^align]`, `[^benchmark]`), plus pre-answers. Claim-level dependence on
a shared lexicon forces indirection; self-contained claims don't.

## Refuted (the causal edge, overstated)

**"Doesn't need qualification" is too strong.** Cairn's belief needed the same
qualification *kinds* bond footnotes — the formal ancestor carried `[^status]`,
`[^timing]`, `[^equal]`, `[^attack]`, near-twins of bond's surviving set. That
qualification was not eliminated by tight scope; it was **relocated** into
`FALSIFICATION.md` as inline prose. The honest form of the claim: cairn's
qualification is *belief-level* (four items, stated once, fits as prose in the formal
sibling), while bond's is *claim-level* (per-claim lexicon and carve-outs), and only
claim-level qualification forces the footnote mechanism into the plain lens.

## Disposition

No document changes required — the live substrate already embodies the surviving form
(0 footnotes in both cairn registers, qualification homed as prose in the formal
sibling). Recorded as grounding for C6 (terms glossed inline), C20 (economy), and C13
(proof of origin at the document level, no footnote layer required in the plain
