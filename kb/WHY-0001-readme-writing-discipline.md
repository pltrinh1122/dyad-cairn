## Why this discipline exists

The root `README.md` is the dyad's only outward-facing surface. Every other artifact —
`DYAD.md`, the kb, the ledger — is read by the dyad itself; the README is read by
strangers, skeptics, and other dyads. It is where proof of origin meets the public.
Drift there is not private drift: a falsifier whose extension silently widens in a
register edit stakes the dyad on claims it never held; honesty fields that soften make
a hypothesis read as settled doctrine.

Two failure modes were actually lived, and each cost real turns:

1. **Form before spine** (2026-07-08, materialization arc — `DYAD_LEDGER.md`, REFLECT
   `2026-07-08 21:07:16`): the Agent drafted large prose from generative interpolation
   of the normative ground, forcing the Operator into the role of a line-editor. The
   Operator's correction — *"provide elicitation questions so that you're 90% aligned
   with the next draft rather than this line-by-line editing"* — is the direct origin
   of the discipline's spine-before-form condition (HOW-0006 C11). This failure mode attacks `craft_telos`
   head-on (dyad-cairn's charter metric, `DYAD.md § Craft`: minimize the discovery +
   structuring turns to turn a messy challenge into a proven skill): it maximizes
   structuring turns instead of minimizing them.

2. **Register edits rewriting claim extensions** (2026-07-08, refinement arc — PR
   #154, audit workflow `wf_8918028a-253`): a plain-language paraphrase, performed
   carefully and reviewed pass-by-pass, still drifted four falsifiers (dropped a
   *generative* qualifier, dropped Operator-routing, collapsed a three-axis bond to
   two, inverted the direction of the equal-refutes falsifier). None of the drift was
   visible to the writer in the act of writing. Only a claim-by-claim adversarial
   audit against the canonical anchors caught it. Hence the discipline's rule: the
   grounding audit is a **gate**, not a courtesy pass.

## Why conciseness is a condition (added 2026-07-13)

Unlike the two failure modes above, conciseness did not enter through a lived failure;
it entered by Operator directive (session d-start, 2026-07-13) and is graded n=0 in
HOW-0006 (C20) accordingly. Its grounding is the charter, not an arc: Condensation is
this dyad's Generate mechanism (`DYAD.md § 3. Form-Grounding`), and the Stone — the
form the README projects — is by definition "highly compressed" (`DYAD.md § 6 & 7.
Ontology & Vocabulary`). A projection that bloats what its source compresses is
unfaithful in the same family as one that drifts a falsifier: the stranger and the
skeptic the README exists for pay attention for every sentence, and prose that does no
work dilutes the claims that do.

## Why a discipline and not a checklist

The failure modes split cleanly along the Builder-vs-Enforcer line:

- Judgments that are **generative** (register, metaphor choice, emphasis, taxonomy)
  belong to the Operator-steered passes of the playbook. No script can hold them.
- Judgments that are **mechanical** (frontmatter schema, honesty-field lock,
  citation resolution, claim/falsifier pairing) belong to `skills/readme_lint.py`,
  paired with its deterministic test suite per the No-Pure-G invariant (`DYAD.md §
  NON-NEGOTIABLE`: no pure Generative execution — every generated script or logic block
  must be immediately paired with a deterministic automated test suite).

Burying either kind in the other destroys the bond: a linter that enforces taste
becomes a false authority; a playbook that hand-checks schemas invites the exact
silent drift it exists to prevent.

## Why it is safe for other dyads to adopt

The README is declared `kind: derived-view` — a lens, never a content-home. The
discipline therefore never asks a dyad to author truth in the README; it asks the
dyad to *project* truth that already lives in its canonical home (`DYAD.md` or
equivalent) and to keep the projection faithful. A dyad that adopts the playbook
inherits the form; its claims, falsifiers, and proof of origin remain entirely its
own. This mirrors how `dyad-cairn` inherited the falsifiable-manifesto form from
`dyad-bond`'s README without inheriting a single claim.
